User Group 2: Hardcore

Description

The user is cosmopolitan fanatic of film. He is financially and socially stable. He has completed some degree of education, and actively engages in critique and objective perception (of films he watches, for instance). He is expecting an efficient experience that quickly provides him film details and showing times.

Pre-Interview Assumptions

I assume this user group would want to explore a lot of the detail of the films that are on the site. Users who are passionate about film would frequently visit the theater, and then frequently visit the site looking at film detail and showing times. He is also interested in cinema membership benefits and involvement. He may also be interested in special events or special showings.

He would seek after various sources of information: what is playing, what time, who directed this film, how long is the film, who are the feature cast members, how can I compliment or critique the cinema, and how can I become a member?

The user did not surprise me very much. My assumptions were correct mostly. Except, as I did not expect, this user type is very open to thinking on his intuition rather than only thinking about his decisions.

Post-Interview Reflections

While hardcore users like this are a minority of movie-theater-goers, I learned that there's still significant differences between them and casual-goers that should be addressed in a site. For one, they have extensive informational needs about a particular film and want to be connected to other resources for that film. They also want to view opportunities to be involved with the theater or express some sort of concern. I can also speculate that professional critics would be within this group, which raises their importance to a very high degree as a user, given that they may rate the films and theaters that they attend, and so must have a rich user experience.

User Scenarios

  1. Max wants to find see who directed Gravity, currently playing at our theater. So Max needs navigational or search elements to (a) find Gravity, (b) see the details of the film, and (c) find the Director detail in the details area.
  2. Max wants to see what films are showing at the theater after 9:00PM tonight. So Max needs to view films based on the date, and clearly see viewing times of the films.
  3. Max wants to contact the theater to bring up a concern he has with the service. So Max needs to find a Contact Us page of some sort, and be able to submit feedback and/or be given other means of contact for the theater.
  4. Max seeks to know the benefits of becoming a member at the theater. Max needs to see a place to register for benefits or see the benefits of membership briefly and efficiently explained.
  5. Max wonders if the theater is providing any special events in the near future. So Max needs to see Events somewhere noticeable from the front page and above the fold, and be led to some sort of explanation or list of events or special showings.
